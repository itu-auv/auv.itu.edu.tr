FROM DESIGN TO PRODUCTION

  • Home
  • Blog

Designing an underwater vehicle is not as easy as it seems. But it is not difficult either. It depends on what kind of underwater vehicle you want to design and for what purpose you want to design it.

To build a vehicle from scratch, you must first have carefully design everything. It is necessary to start with the basic equipment that should be in the vehicle and draw a design plan from the inside of the vehicle to the very outside. While we were designing our vehicle, we started to design a chamber that would contain these parts, considering the electronic parts that should be in our vehicle. We decided to make an acrylic tube for our electronic chamber because it has a large area and is easily available in the market. After the dimensions of the tube were determined, we thought about how we could mount the motors that will provide the basic movement of our vehicle, and as a result, we created a skeleton around the tube using sigma profiles. After creating the skeleton of the vehicle, it came to designing the mission equipment such as the rack for the tube inside, the areas where the mission equipment will be located outside the tube, and the thrusters.

All of our designs were made through Autodesk's Fusion 360 program. The design maintains its continuity as the team continues to work, it never ends, but after the best result, it is necessary to start production. We started production with tubes and skeletons. The remaining parts were produced according to the financial means we had. First the easiest and cheapest parts were produced, and then more expensive parts were produced with the help of sponsors. Many parts such as rack, pusher, mission equipment, fasteners in skeletons were 3D printed in our tool room.
